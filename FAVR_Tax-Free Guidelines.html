Tax-Free Guidelines

All reimbursements are paid entirely tax-free. There is no tax withholding and no W2 reporting, if you adhere to ALL the following requirements:


  1. MSRP (sticker price) plus Sales Tax on your vehicle, when it was new, equaled at least the price of the standard vehicle used in your rate calculation. This is regardless of what you have actually paid for the vehicle and whether you purchased the vehicle new or used.

  2. The model year of your vehicle must not differ from the current calendar year by more than the number of years in your retention period.

  3. You must carry at least the minimum insurance limits listed on your company’s Reimbursement Policy.

  4. You must drive a minimum of 5,000 business miles per 12-month period. If you are not enrolled in the Reimbursement Program for a full 12 months, the 5,000-business mile requirement will be prorated according to your start date.

  5. You must not have claimed additional depreciation on your vehicle, using IRS Section 179 deductions, ACRS-Accelerated Cost Recovery System, or MACRS-Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System.

  6. If you receive reimbursement for months when you are not an active employee (i.e. leave of absence), any reimbursement amount paid during that period must be treated as income and taxed accordingly.


    If you fail to meet any one of these criteria, a percentage of your total reimbursement may become subject to tax. To determine taxability, your monthly business miles will be multiplied by the current IRS rate. This will represent how much of your monthly reimbursement may be paid to you tax-free. Business Mileage x IRS Standard Rate = Maximum tax-free Reimbursement. The balance of your reimbursement, if there is a balance, will then become subject to tax.


    Example A:

    • Total Annual Reimbursement = $11,000

    • Total Annual Business Mileage = 18,000

    • Maximum Tax-Free Reimbursement: 18,000 x 65.5 cents = $11,790

    • Annual Reimbursement $11,000 < $11,790 Maximum Tax-Free Reimbursement

    • No Taxable Income


      Example B:

    • Total Annual Reimbursement = $12,000

    • Total Annual Business Mileage = 18,000

    • Maximum Tax-Free Reimbursement: 18,000 x 65.5 cents = $11,790

    • Annual Reimbursement $11,500 > $11,790 Maximum Tax-Free Reimbursement

    • $210 = Taxable Income


The IRS standard rate: https://www.irs.gov/tax-professionals/standard-mileage-rates
