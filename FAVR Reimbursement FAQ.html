FAVR: Fixed and Variable Rate Reimbursement Program


Q: What is FAVR?

A: FAVR stands for Fixed and Variable Rate. FAVR is an IRS approved vehicle reimbursement program intended to reimburse you for the business usage of driving your personal vehicle for business.


Q: What does FAVR Reimbursement include?

A: FAVR Reimburses for the fixed and variable business expenses related to driving a personal vehicle for business use. The Reimbursement consists of a monthly fixed payment and a variable cent per mile rate.


Q: What does FAVR cover?

A: The periodic fixed payment is to reimburse you for vehicle related fixed costs including depreciation, insurance, registration fees, and personal property taxes. The payment is calculated using a benchmark vehicle and localized to your zip code. The periodic variable payment is intended to reimburse for the variable operating costs including fuel, oil changes, tires, and routine maintenance. The payment is calculated using a benchmark vehicle and localized to your zip code.


Q: Are the costs used in the calculation for my actual costs on my personal vehicle?

A: No, your employer has chosen a base vehicle which is suitable for performing your job function. Your reimbursement payments are calculated using this base vehicle to determine the cost of doing business in your living area. You are reimbursed as if you are driving the benchmark vehicle for business. Your employer may benchmark off multiple vehicles to meet different job functions or positions.


Q: Does my mileage impact my FAVR Reimbursement?

A: Yes, you will be placed into a mileage band that is used to calculate the depreciation for the benchmark vehicle. High mileage drivers incur faster depreciation on their vehicle and will receive higher fixed rates than low mileage drivers. Movement between bands based on actual mileage will adjust the fixed portion of the reimbursement.


Q: What else impacts my FAVR payment?

A: The fixed and variable rates take into consideration the specific area in which the employee is located and the associated vehicle and operating costs including taxes, license and registration, insurance, and fuel.


Q: Are rental cars eligible for Reimbursement?

A: No, you cannot enroll a rental car into the Reimbursement program. If you are required to driver a rental or loaner vehicle while active on the Reimbursement program, you are not

required to enroll the rental, but you can continue to log business mileage through Wheels. You can enter in ‘1’ as the odometer reading while reporting mileage for the rental.


Q: Can I drive multiple vehicles?

A: No, you are expected to drive one vehicle primarily for business. This is the vehicle you enroll into the Wheels Reimbursement Program. On occasion you may be required to use a rental or loaner while your vehicle is in for maintenance. If the vehicle is insured, you can continue to track and submit business miles through Wheels. You can enter ‘1’ for the odometer upon submitting miles.


Q: Can I lease a vehicle?

A: Yes, driver owned or leased vehicles are eligible for program enrollment.


Q: Does Wheels offer assistance in locating a vehicle or discounts on services?

A: No, not at this time.


Program Compliance


Q: Is FAVR tax free?

A: FAVR is provided to eligible employees on a tax-free basis when drivers comply with the program guidelines. Tax-free reimbursement is dependent on compliance with the tests outlined by the IRS related to age of the vehicle, price of the vehicle, insurance coverage, and business mileage. If any one of these IRS guidelines is not met, a percentage of your total reimbursement may become subject to tax.


  • Vehicle Age: The vehicle driven cannot be older than the number of years used in the “retention cycle” used when determining tax-free compliance.


  • Vehicle Cost: MSRP (sticker price) plus Sales Tax on your vehicle, when it was new, equaled at least 90% of the new car price of the program base vehicle used to establish your FAVR rates. This is regardless of what you may have actually paid for the vehicle and whether or not you purchased the vehicle new or used.


  • Insurance Coverage: Drivers are responsible for securing insurance for the vehicle that will be used for business purposes. The cost of insurance is addressed in the fixed portion of your reimbursement. You must carry at least the minimum insurance limits required by your employer.


  • Business Mileage: You must drive a minimum of 5,000 business miles per 12-month period. If you are not enrolled in FAVR for a full 12 months, the 5,000 business mile requirement will be prorated according to your start date.

  • You must not have claimed additional depreciation on your vehicle, using IRS Section 179 deductions, ACRS-Accelerated Cost Recovery System, or MACRS-Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System.


Q: Why does my company’s vehicle age requirement different than the IRS requirement? A: The IRS requires your vehicle to be a certain age to remain tax compliant. Once your vehicle exceeds the vehicle age used to calculate your payment, your vehicle is considered fully depreciated. If your employer allows you to remain on the FAVR program, your reimbursements in excess of the standard IRS Reimbursement rate may be taxable.


Q: How is vehicle age calculated?

A: There are two ways to calculate vehicle age compliance. In example 1, we are looking at vehicles compliant for current calendar year. In example 2, we are looking at how long your vehicle will remain compliant on the program. Please note the examples use 3 Years, however, this value should be substituted with the number of years in your specific mileage band.

  1. Current Year – Mileage Band Year = Current Year Compliance. Example: 2023 – 3 Years

    = 2020. All vehicles model year 2020 and newer will qualify for the FAVR program through December 31st of 2023.

  2. Vehicle Model Year + Mileage Band Year = Year Qualified through. Example: MY 2023 + 3 Years = 2026. The vehicle will qualify through 12/31/2026.


Q: If I am out of compliance, how is the taxable benefit calculated?

A: For vehicles out of compliance, if your total reimbursement payments exceed the amount of your total business mileage multiplied by the current IRS standard reimbursement rate than the overage will be taxable.


Q: How often is vehicle compliance validated?

A: Vehicle compliance for age is reviewed annually. Compliance for value is determined at enrollment, enrollment of a new vehicle or program change. Insurance is reviewed at enrollment and each expiration date. Mileage is reviewed annually.


Q: How often is the taxable benefit applied?

A: Compliance is reviewed and applied quarterly. Please refer to your company’s policy for your company’s schedule.


Reimbursement Disbursement


Q: Why didn’t I get my fixed payment this month?

A: Failure to submit business miles on a frequent basis or lapse in proof of insurance may result in the stopping of your fixed payment.

Q: Why didn’t I get my full fixed this month?

A: The first month may be prorated because on your start dates. Your fixed payment will be prorated based on the start date provided to Wheels by your company.


Q: When will I receive my reimbursement?

A: Reimbursements are issued in the arrears of the month incurred. Reimbursements disbursed via direct deposit through Wheels will be issued on the 20th of each month excluding weekends and holidays. Reimbursements issued via your company’s payroll will be issued on the last pay period of each month.


Q: What if I am on Leave of Absence, do I still get paid?

A: Fixed payments will be suspended until employees return from the leave. The fixed reimbursement portion will be prorated the month the leave of absence begins and the month of return. Please refer to your company’s policy to determine how LOA is managed.


Wheels Mobile Assistant

Q: What permissions do I need to give to run the mobile app on my phone?

A: You must give the app full permissions to location services on your phone. Typically, these can be set under your phone’s Settings > Apps > Wheels > Permissions > Location.


Q: How do I know the app is running?

A: It’s a good idea to check the app periodically to ensure you are logged in and the app is running. App upgrades may require you to log back into the app.


Q: How do I know if the GPS is tracking?

A: You must enable GPS and select a tracking method for GPS to track trips. Once activated, a tracking status will display on the home screen. If your GPS has ‘fallen asleep’ after extended period of non-use; you can reactivate it by switching to manual tracking for a couple trips and then set it back to your preferred tracking method.


Q: Will I be able to edit, delete, or merge trips?

A: Trip log functionality includes the ability to add trips manually or track mileage via GPS. All trips added or tracked can be edited, deleted or merged to one trip prior to submitting for reimbursement.


Q: How does the App track me when I do not have a cell phone signal?

A: A cell phone signal is required to track trips via GPS. If you start a trip with signal and lose it during a trip, they trip may be recorded but will need to be edited for the part of the route that is not tracked. If you do not have a cell phone signal for the entire trip, you will need to add the trip

manually. When the GPS is activated, there is an indicator on the home page that displays the status of tracking.


Q: Can the mobile app submit my miles at the end of the month automatically?

A: No, submission is not automatic. You are required to review and edit your mileage for accuracy prior to submitting for reimbursement.


Q: Will GPS tracking when phone battery is low?

A: When your phone goes into power saving mode, it may impact trip tracking via GPS. If possible, plug your phone in while driving.

Q: How can I optimize the GPS tracking?

A: Additional Troubleshooting tips:

  • Ensure Location Services is set to Always

  • Ensure you choose High Accuracy Mode if available

  • Ensure Background Data Usage is enabled

  • Enable Wi-Fi and Bluetooth

  • Enable turn-by-turn guidance with Google Maps, Apple Maps, or Waze
